名詞

1

不可算名詞


a

〔家族・友人・祖国などに対する〕愛，愛情 〔for，of，to，toward〕.


love and hate 愛と憎しみ, 愛憎.
love of (one's) country 愛国心.
love for one's children 子に対する愛情.

b

[通例 one's love] (よろしくという)あいさつ.


Give [Send] my love to… …によろしく(お伝えください).
Mother [My mother] sends her love. 母からもよろしく 《★手紙の文句》.

2

a

不可算名詞 〔異性に対する〕恋愛，恋 〔of，for，to，toward〕.


(one's) first love 初恋.
free love 自由恋愛(論).
love at first sight ひと目ぼれ.
Love is blind. 《諺》 恋は盲目.

b

不可算名詞 性欲，色情; 性交.


c

可算名詞 情事，恋愛.


3

a

不可算名詞 [また a love] 〔物・事に対する〕好み，愛好，趣味 〔of，for〕.


a love of learning 好学心.
have a love of nature 自然を愛好する.
a labor of love ⇒labor 名詞 3.

b

可算名詞 好きなもの[事].


Golf is one of his great loves. ゴルフは彼の大好きなものの一つだ.

4

a

可算名詞 (通例男から見た)恋人 (cf. lover 1).


b

[my love で夫婦間などの呼び掛けに用いて] ねえ; おまえ，あなた.


c

[女同士のまたは女・子供への呼び掛けに用いて] ねえ; あなた，おまえ.


Yes, love. ええ, あなた[おまえ].

5

a

[L] 恋愛の神，キューピッド.


b

[love，L] 可算名詞 キューピッドの絵[像].


6

不可算名詞 (神の)愛，慈悲; 〔神に対する〕敬愛，崇敬 〔of〕.


the love of God＝God's love 神の愛.
love of God 神への愛.

7

可算名詞 《英口語》 愉快な人，きれいなもの[人].


What a love of a dog! まあ何とかわいい犬だろう!

love

愛▷名詞1
恋愛▷名詞2
強い好み▷名詞3
愛する▷他動詞1自動詞
大好きである▷他動詞3

名詞
1〈…に対する〉愛，愛情〈for/of/to/toward〉
  • love of one's country
    愛国心
  • love for mankind
    人類愛
  • show love toward one's neighbors
    近所の人に親しみを示す
  • a labor of love
    （金銭ずくでなく）好きでする仕事
  • A mother's love for her child knows no bounds.
    母親の子どもに対する愛情は限りがない

2恋愛，恋；性欲
  • love at first sight
    一目ぼれ
  • Love is blind.
    ((ことわざ))恋は盲目

3((またa ～))〈…に対する〉強い好み，愛好〈of/for〉
  • a love of learning
    向学心
  • love of music
    音楽を愛する心
  • He has a great love for sports.
    彼はスポーツが大好きである

4大好きな物［事］
  • Fishing is one of his great loves.
    釣りは彼の大好きな事の１つである

5恋をした相手；恋人，愛する人
  • my first love
    私の初恋の人
  • She is the love of his life.
    彼女は彼のすべてだ

6大切な人；((呼びかけに用いて))ねえ，君，あなた（恋人・夫婦・わが子などに対して用いる）
  • Love
    愛をこめて（手紙の結句）
  • Come here, my love!
    ねえ，あなた，ここへ来て

7≪ﾃﾆｽ≫無得点，ゼロ
  • She was ahead 30‐love.
    彼女は30‐0でリードしていた

8((口))かわいいもの
9＝love affair

成句be in love
〈…に〉恋している〈with〉

成句fall in love
〈…を〉恋する，〈…に〉ほれる〈with〉
  • I fell in love with her at first sight.

（通例進行形にはできない）

他動詞
1（人）を愛する，（人）に恋愛感情を抱く
  • “Do you love me?"“Yes, I do love you."
    「私のこと愛している?」「もちろんとても愛しているよ」（doは強意を表す）
  • They loved each other.
    彼らは愛し合っていた

2（家族・祖国など）を敬愛する，かわいがる，大事にする
  • I love my family.
    私は家族を愛しています
  • My grandfather loved his dog more than anything else.
    祖父は何よりも自分の犬をかわいがった

3（物事）が大好きである
  • I do love tea and toast!
    私は紅茶とトーストが大好きだ
  • I love a little brandy before going to bed.
    寝る前にブランデーを少し飲むのが大好きだ
  • You're going to love my room.
    きっと私の部屋が気に入るよ
  • They both loved dancing .
    彼らは２人ともダンスが大好きだった

4《love to do》…したいと思う；《love A to do》A（人）にぜひ…してほしいと思う（would, shouldとともに用いる；女性の好む表現）
  • “Will you please dance with me?"“I'd love to."
    「いっしょに踊っていただけますか」「喜んで」
  • I'd love you to come with me.
    ぜひ私といっしょに来てほしい

自動詞
（人を）愛する，恋をする
  • It is wonderful to be loved; but I think it is much more wonderful to love.
    愛されることはすばらしいが，私は愛することのほうがずっとすばらしいことだと思う

((口))それはすてきだ（しばしば皮肉・反語的）

自動詞

①人を愛する

  • The poor man never knew what it was like to love.
    気の毒なその男は愛するということがどんなものかを知らなかった
  • I have never loved like this before.
    私はこれまでこのように愛したことはなかった

②《love to do》ぜひ...したい

  • I'd love to visit New Nepal.
    ネパールにぜ行ってみたい
  • I'd love for you to come over some time.
    いつかあなたにぜひ来ていただきたい
    《love for A to do》Aにぜひ...してほしい
他動詞

①a...を愛する

  • I love her not only as a sister, but also as a friend.
    私は彼女のことを妹としてだけではなく, 友だちとしても愛しています
  • The sumo wrestler was so well-loved that thousands came to cheer him on.
    その相撲取りはとても愛されていたので何千人もが声援に来た
  • People rushed to the scene of the accident to find their loved ones.
    人々は愛する人たちを見つけるために事故の現場に急行し 駆け付けた
  • You can't force someone to love his own country.
    誰かにその祖国を愛することを強いることはできない

①b...に恋する

  • The more I know you, the more I love you.
    あなたのことを知れば知るほど愛します 愛が深まります
  • She's the woman I love.
    彼女が私の愛する 恋する女性です
  • Love me little, love me long.
    少し愛して, 長く愛して

②...が大好きである

  • Pete loves listening to all kinds of music.
    ピートはあらゆる種類の音楽を聞くのが大好きだ
  • Your mother would love this book.
    君のお母さんはこの本が気に入ると思う
  • I love to cook, but I love to eat even more.
    私は料理をするのが大好きですが, 食べることはもっと好きです
  • I love the way you talk.
    私はあなたがお話しする仕方が好きです
  • You're going to love this joke.
    この冗談は気に入るよ
  • Your dad's gonna love you when he finds out what you've done.
    (皮肉を込めて)あなたがしたことを知ったらお父さんはとっても喜ぶわよ

③《love to do》...したいと思う

  • "Would you like to go out for some pizza?" "I'd love to!"
    「ピザを食べに行きたい?」「喜んで!」
  • I'd love to have been at that concert.
    私はあのコンサートに行けてたらよかったのに
  • I'd love to go abroad some day.
    いつか外国に行きたい

④...を好む

  • Some cats love milk, other don't.
    ある猫はミルクを好み, 他の猫は好まない
句動詞

気に入った

  • I can't believe you did that -- I love it!

love

English phrasebook
This entry is part of the phrasebook project, which presents criteria for inclusion based on usefulness, simplicity and commonness.

発音

  • (英国発音, 米国発音) IPA(key): /lʌv/
  • (Northern England) IPA(key): /lʊːv/
  • (Ireland) IPA(key): /lʊv/
  • 韻: -ʌv

語源 1

From Middle English love, luve, from 古期英語 lufu (“love, affection, desire”), from Proto-Germanic *lubō (“love”), from Proto-Indo-European *lewbʰ- (“love, care, desire”).

The closing-of-a-letter sense is presumably a truncation of With love or the like.

The verb is from Middle English loven, lovien, from 古期英語 lufian (“to love, cherish, sow love to; fondle, caress; delight in, approve, practice”), from the noun lufu (“love”), see above.

名詞

love (countable かつ uncountable, 複数形 loves)

  1. (uncountable) Strong affection.
    1. A profound and caring affection towards someone.
      A mother’s love is not easily shaken.
      My husband’s love is the most important thing in my life.
      • 1674, John Milton, Paradise Lost:
        He on his side / Leaning half-raised, with looks of cordial love / Hung over her enamoured.
      • 2014, S. Hidden, Jewish, Christian, and Islamic Mystical Perspectives on the Love of God (→ISBN)
    2. Affectionate, benevolent concern or care for other people or beings, and for their well-being.
      • 1864, Utilitarianism Explained and Exemplified in Moral and Political Government:
        The love of your neighbor as yourself, is expressly given as the definition and test of Charity,—not alms-giving—and this love is [...] the highest of all the Divine commands[.]
    3. A feeling of intense attraction towards someone.
      I have never been in love as much as I have with you.
      • 1898, Winston Churchill, chapter 8, in The Celebrity:
        The humor of my proposition appealed more strongly to Miss Trevor than I had looked for, and from that time forward she became her old self again; for, even after she had conquered her love for the Celebrity, the mortification of having been jilted by him remained.
    4. A deep or abiding liking for something; an enthusiasm for something.
      My love of cricket knows no bounds.
      • 2012, Philip Auerswald, The Coming Prosperity (→ISBN):
        For three decades, the average number of miles driven by US motorists increased steadily. Then, in 2007, that steady climb was suddenly halted. [...] What magic caused Americans to temper their longstanding love of the open road?
  2. (countable) A person who is the object of romantic feelings; a darling, a sweetheart, a beloved.
    • (Can we date this quote?) Edmund Spenser
      Open the temple gates unto my love.
    • 1596-97, William Shakespeare, The Merchant of Venice, Act III Scene 2
      O love, dispatch all business, and be gone!
    • 1969, The Dubliners, Dirty Old Town
      I met my love by the gasworks wall.
  3. (colloquial, Britain) A term of friendly address, regardless of feelings.
    Hello love, how can I help you?
  4. A thing, activity, etc which is the object of one's deep liking or enthusiasm.
    • 1997 March, "Faces of Today's Black Woman", in Ebony, volume 52, number 5, page 96:
      But it wasn't until [Theresa M. Claiborne] went to ROTC training camp at the University of California at Berkeley that she discovered that flying was her first love. "Pilots talk about getting bit by the flying bug," she says. "I thought, This is heaven."
  5. (euphemistic) Sexual desire; attachment based on sexual attraction.
    • 2013, Ronald Long, Men, Homosexuality, and the Gods, Routledge (→ISBN), page 3:
      The prospect that their cherished Greeks would have countenanced, much less honored, a love between men that expressed itself carnally, however, was not so easily assimilated.
  6. (euphemistic) Sexual activity.
    • 1986, Ben Elton & al., Blackadder II, "Bells":
      —What think you, my lord, of... love?
      —You mean ‘rumpy-pumpy’.
  7. An instance or episode of being in love; a love affair.
    • (Can we date this quote?), E. L. Todd, Then Came Absolution (→ISBN):
      Maybe it was just a summer love, something with no future.
  8. Used as the closing, before the signature, of a letter, especially between good friends or family members, or by the young.
  9. Alternative letter-case form of Love (“personification of love”).
    • c. 1810,, Samuel Johnson (in The Works of Samuel Johnson):
      At busy hearts in vain love's arrows fly; [...]
  10. (obsolete) A thin silk material.
    • 1664, Robert Boyle, Experiments and Considerations Touching Colours, […]
      Such a kind of transparency, as that of a Sive, a piece of Cyprus, or a Love-Hood.
  11. A climbing plant, Clematis vitalba.
同意語
  • (darling, sweetheart): baby, darling, lover, pet, sweetheart, honey, love bird
  • (term of address): mate, lover, darling, sweetie, sweetheart
反意語
  • (strong affection): hate, hatred, angst; malice, spite
  • (absence of love): indifference

動詞

love (三人称単数 現在形 loves, 現在分詞 loving, 過去形および過去分詞形 loved)

  1. (usually transitive, sometimes intransitive, stative) To have a strong affection for (someone または something).
    • 1918, Edgar Rice Burroughs, The Land That Time Forgot Chapter VI
      I wanted to take her in my arms and tell her how I loved her, and had taken her hand from the rail and started to draw her toward me when Olson came blundering up on deck with his bedding.
    • 2013 February 26, Pink and Nate Ruess, Just Give Me a Reason:
      Just give me a reason, / just a little bit's enough, / just a second we're not broken, just bent / and we can learn to love again.
    I love my spouse.   I love you!
  2. (transitive) To need, thrive on.
    Mold loves moist, dark places.
  3. (transitive) To be strongly inclined towards something; an emphatic form of like.
    I love walking barefoot on wet grass;  I'd love to join the team;  I love what you've done with your hair
  4. (usually transitive, sometimes intransitive) To care deeply about, to be dedicated to (someone または something).
    • John 3:16
      For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life.
    • Matthew: 37-38
      You shall love the Lord your God with your whole heart, and your whole mind, and your whole soul; you shall love your neighbor as yourself.
    • 2013 June 21, Oliver Burkeman, “The tao of tech”, in The Guardian Weekly, volume 189, number 2, page 27:
      The dirty secret of the internet is that all this distraction and interruption is immensely profitable. Web companies like to boast about […] offering services that let you […] "share the things you love with the world" and so on. But the real way to build a successful online business is to be better than your rivals at undermining people's control of their own attention.
  5. (transitive) To derive delight from a fact or situation.
    I love the fact that the coffee shop now offers fat-free chai latte.
  6. (transitive) To lust for.
  7. (transitive, euphemistic) To have sex with (perhaps from make love).
    I wish I could love her all night long.

語源 2

From Middle English loven, lovien, from 古期英語 lofian (“to praise, exalt, appraise, value”), from Proto-Germanic *lubōną (“to praise, vow”), from *lubą (“praise”), from Proto-Indo-European *lewbʰ- (“to like, love, desire”), *lewbʰ-.

動詞

love (三人称単数 現在形 loves, 現在分詞 loving, 過去形および過去分詞形 loved)

  1. (transitive, obsolete or Britain dialectal) To praise; commend.
  2. (transitive, obsolete or Britain dialectal) To praise as of value; prize; set a price on.

語源 3

Said by some to be from the idea that when one does a thing “for love”, that is for no monetary gain, the word “love” implies "nothing". The previously held belief that it originated from the French term l’œuf (“the egg”), due to its shape, is no longer widely accepted, though compare the use of duck (reputed to be short for duck's egg) for a zero score at cricket.

名詞

love (uncountable)

  1. (racquet sports, billiards) Zero, no score.
    So that’s fifteen-love to Kournikova.
    • 2013, Paul McNamee, Game Changer: My Tennis Life
      The next day Agassi came back from two sets to love down to beat Courier in five sets.

アナグラム
